Brief Introduction to Sun Salutation

About

Sun Salutation, also known as Surya Namaskar in Sanskrit, is a series of yoga poses that flow together, traditionally performed at sunrise to greet the sun and its energy. This sequence, which varies slightly among yoga schools, is a fundamental practice in many yoga styles and is often used as a warm-up.



Source:Yoga Vectors by Vecteezy

Key aspects of Sun Salutation:

  • Fluid movement: Each pose flows seamlessly into the next, creating a continuous movement.
  • Breath synchronization: The sequence is typically synchronized with the breath, with each inhale or exhale corresponding to a specific pose.
  • Honor the sun: The practice acknowledges the sun as a source of life and energy.
  • Physical and mental benefits: Sun Salutation can improve flexibility, strength, cardiovascular health, and mental clarity.
  • Variations: Different yoga lineages may have slightly different variations of the Sun Salutation sequence.

Sequence of Sun Salutation:

  1. Pranamasana (Mountain Pose Namaste)
  2. Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)
  3. Uttanasana (Standing Forward Fold Pose)
  4. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Crescent Low Lunge Pose Variation Knee On Floor)
  5. Phalakasana (Plank Pose)
  6. Ashtanga Namaskara (Eight Limbed Pose)
  7. Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)
  8. Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward Facing Dog Pose)
  9. Ashwa Sanchalanasana (Crescent Low Lunge Pose Variation Knee On Floor)
  10. Uttanasana (Standing Forward Fold Pose)
  11. Hasta Uttanasana (Raised Arms Pose)
